Patient education

Shoulder Care

Shoulder pain can come from a rotator cuff tear, arthritis, instability, bursitis, tendonitis, fracture, or an injury that never fully recovered. Dr. Barnes evaluates the cause of pain and builds a plan around your goals, exam, imaging, and activity level.

Treatment options

Most orthopedic problems deserve a thoughtful discussion of both non-surgical and surgical options. Depending on the diagnosis, treatment may include:

  • Physical therapy and home exercise programs
  • Anti-inflammatory strategies when appropriate
  • Corticosteroid injections
  • Ultrasound-guided injections when appropriate
  • Arthroscopic rotator cuff repair
  • Shoulder stabilization/labral repair
  • Shoulder replacement and reverse shoulder replacement

What to expect

Dr. Barnes' approach is to explain what is causing the problem, what can be tried without surgery, when surgery becomes reasonable, and what recovery usually looks like.

This section will continue to expand with specific condition explainers, surgical guides, recovery timelines, and frequently asked questions.
When should I schedule an appointment?

Schedule an evaluation when pain limits sleep, work, walking, lifting, sports, or daily activities, or when an injury is not improving as expected.

Will I need surgery?

Not always. Many orthopedic conditions improve with non-surgical care. Surgery is considered when symptoms, imaging, exam findings, and goals support it.

Can I bring imaging?

Yes. Bring prior X-rays, MRI reports, operative notes, injection records, and therapy notes when available.
