Common shoulder conditions treated.

Soft-tissue problems, arthritis, instability and trauma can overlap. The evaluation helps sort out what is actually driving pain and loss of function.

💪 Tendon, biceps and soft-tissue problems

💪

Rotator Cuff Tears

Pain, weakness, difficulty lifting the arm and night pain. Tears may be traumatic or degenerative.

🔥

Impingement & Bursitis

Pain reaching overhead, reaching behind the back or sleeping on the shoulder.

🎣

Biceps Tendinitis & Rupture

Pain in the front of the shoulder, pain with lifting, or a visible “Popeye” deformity after rupture.

🎯

Scapular Dyskinesia

Abnormal shoulder blade mechanics that can contribute to pain, weakness and performance problems.

🦴 Arthritis, instability and joint problems

🦴

Shoulder Arthritis

Glenohumeral arthritis and AC joint arthritis can cause stiffness, pain and loss of function.

🔁

Labral Tears & Instability

Bankart tears, bony Bankart injuries, recurrent dislocations and feelings of slipping or apprehension.

🏈

AC Separations & AC Pain

AC joint arthritis, separations and pain at the top of the shoulder after injury or overuse.

🩻 Trauma and fractures

🩻

Clavicle Fractures

Collarbone fractures from falls, sports injuries, cycling crashes and other trauma.

🦾

Proximal Humerus Fractures

Fractures near the ball of the shoulder, often after a fall or higher-energy injury.

🚗

Shoulder Injury After Accident

Shoulder pain after falls, collisions, seatbelt injuries, water sports, ATV crashes or sports trauma.

Understanding the shoulder.

The shoulder is a highly mobile joint. Pain may come from the joint surface, tendons, labrum, biceps tendon, AC joint, bone or shoulder blade mechanics.

Rotator Cuff

The tendon group that helps lift, rotate and stabilize the arm.

Labrum

A cartilage rim that helps deepen the socket and can tear with instability.

Biceps Tendon

A common source of anterior shoulder pain and inflammation.

AC Joint

The small joint at the top of the shoulder that can develop arthritis or separation.

Glenohumeral Joint

The main ball-and-socket shoulder joint affected by arthritis and instability.

Clavicle & Humerus

Bone injuries can range from collarbone fractures to proximal humerus fractures.

Treatment options

Start with the right diagnosis, then build the plan.

Most shoulder problems deserve a thoughtful discussion of both nonsurgical and surgical options. The right plan depends on the diagnosis, severity, imaging findings, activity goals, age, health and response to prior care.

✓ Activity modification, therapy and home exercise plans
✓ Cortisone injections, ultrasound-guided injections and orthobiologics when appropriate
✓ Arthroscopic shoulder procedures, fracture care and shoulder replacement when surgery is indicated

Shoulder procedures.

Patient-friendly illustrations show the problem, the repair concept and the goal of surgery when surgery is the right choice.

Arthroscopic tendon repair

Rotator Cuff Repair

Rotator cuff repair is used for selected tears that cause pain, weakness, night pain and loss of overhead function.

  • Often performed arthroscopically through small incisions
  • Protects the repair early, then gradually restores motion and strength
  • Recovery depends on tear size, tissue quality and healing biology
Minimally invasive shoulder surgery

Shoulder Arthroscopy

Shoulder arthroscopy uses a camera and specialized instruments to evaluate and treat selected problems inside the shoulder.

  • Small incisions rather than a large open exposure for many conditions
  • May include decompression, debridement, labral repair or cuff repair
  • Procedure choice depends on the diagnosis and goals
Instability procedure

Labral / Bankart Repair

For selected instability patterns, the torn labrum and capsule may be repaired to improve shoulder stability.

  • Used for recurrent dislocation or persistent apprehension in selected patients
  • May address soft-tissue Bankart or bony Bankart patterns
  • Return-to-sport is staged and protection matters early
Anterior shoulder pain

Biceps Tenodesis

Biceps tenodesis may be considered when the long head of the biceps tendon is a major pain generator or is involved with other shoulder pathology.

  • Commonly combined with other arthroscopic shoulder procedures when indicated
  • Aims to reduce biceps-related pain while preserving useful function
  • Restrictions early protect the healing tendon fixation
Arthritis surgery

Shoulder Replacement

Shoulder replacement may be considered for advanced shoulder arthritis, severe pain, stiffness and loss of function when nonoperative care is no longer enough.

  • May include anatomic or reverse shoulder replacement depending on the shoulder
  • Planning depends on arthritis pattern, rotator cuff status and bone quality
  • The goal is pain relief and improved function for daily life
Fracture care

Clavicle Fracture Fixation

Many clavicle fractures heal without surgery, but selected displaced, shortened or multi-fragment fractures may benefit from plate and screw fixation.

  • Decision depends on displacement, skin risk, activity needs and fracture pattern
  • Fixation restores alignment and supports healing
  • Some healing produces a callus bump even when treated without surgery
