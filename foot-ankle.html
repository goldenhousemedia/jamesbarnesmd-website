Foot & Ankle Care

Foot and ankle pain can limit walking, work, exercise, sports and daily activity. This page is an educational starting point for ankle sprains, fractures, Achilles problems, tendon injuries, arthritis and other foot and ankle concerns.

Treatment options

Most orthopedic problems deserve a thoughtful discussion of both non-surgical and surgical options. Depending on the diagnosis, treatment may include:

  • Activity modification and shoe-wear guidance
  • Bracing, boot immobilization or supportive devices
  • Physical therapy and strengthening
  • Anti-inflammatory care when appropriate
  • Injections when appropriate
  • Fracture care
  • Tendon repair when indicated
  • Referral when subspecialty foot and ankle care is needed

What to expect

Dr. Barnes' approach is to explain what is causing the problem, what can be tried without surgery, when surgery becomes reasonable, and what recovery usually looks like.

This section will continue to expand with specific foot and ankle condition explainers, recovery timelines, surgical guides and frequently asked questions.
When should I schedule an appointment?

Schedule an evaluation when pain limits walking, standing, work, sports or daily activity, or when an injury is not improving as expected.

Do ankle sprains need X-rays?

Some do. Inability to bear weight, significant swelling, deformity, bone tenderness or persistent pain may require imaging.

Can I bring imaging?

Yes. Bring prior X-rays, MRI reports, operative notes, injection records and therapy notes when available.
