When should you see an orthopedic surgeon?

Orthopedic evaluation is useful when pain, swelling, stiffness, weakness, numbness, instability, injury, or arthritis is limiting daily life and the diagnosis or next step is unclear.

Diagnosis and imaging review

Many patients come in after X-rays, MRI, CT, urgent care, or a prior opinion and need the findings translated into a practical plan.

Nonoperative care first when reasonable

Surgery is not the default answer. Bracing, therapy, injections, medications, activity changes, and time may be appropriate depending on the diagnosis.

Second opinions

A second opinion can clarify timing, surgical options, recovery expectations, and whether local care is appropriate.

Local orthopedic care for the region.

Patients commonly look for orthopedic care close to home rather than traveling to Phoenix, Las Vegas, or larger centers when local evaluation and treatment are appropriate.

Serving surrounding communities

Dr. Barnes sees patients from Lake Havasu City, Desert Hills, Parker, Bullhead City, Kingman, Needles, Mohave County, La Paz County, and nearby communities.

When referral may be better

Complex revision surgery, major deformity reconstruction, severe infection, unusually high medical risk, or highly specialized procedures may be better handled with a subspecialist or tertiary center.

Orthopedic care for Lake Havasu patients who want clear options.

Patients often start with pain, stiffness, swelling, weakness, limited activity, or uncertainty about whether surgery is necessary. The first job is to identify the problem and explain reasonable treatment options.

Common reasons to schedule

  • Hip, knee, shoulder, hand, foot, or ankle pain
  • Arthritis and joint replacement questions
  • Sports injuries and return-to-activity concerns
  • Fracture care and injury evaluation
  • Second opinions before committing to surgery
  • Injections, bracing, therapy, and nonoperative planning

Shared decision-making

The goal is not to push every patient toward surgery. The goal is to review the history, exam, imaging, and priorities, then decide whether nonsurgical care, injections, therapy, bracing, activity modification, or surgery makes the most sense.
