Why Do Joints Pop and Crack?

A simple orthopedic explanation of common joint sounds and when symptoms deserve attention.

Bone BlogGeneral OrthopedicsPatient Education

Joints can pop, click, crack, snap, grind, or creak for several reasons. In many people, painless joint noise is common.

Common reasons joints make noise

Joint sounds may come from pressure changes in joint fluid, tendons moving over bone, or rougher cartilage surfaces. The sound alone is often less important than the symptoms around it.

When the sound matters more

Joint popping deserves more attention when it is associated with pain, swelling, catching, locking, giving way, weakness, reduced motion, a new injury, or symptoms that are worsening over time.

Bottom line

A noisy joint does not automatically mean something is wrong. A noisy and painful joint deserves a closer look.

Need a joint checked?

If symptoms are limiting your activity, an orthopedic evaluation can help clarify the cause.
