Hip Pain, Bursitis & Robotic Hip Replacement

Hip pain can quietly shrink your life: shorter walks, poor sleep, trouble with stairs, less golf, less travel and less confidence staying active. Dr. Barnes helps patients sort out arthritis, bursitis, tendon pain, fractures and painful prior hip replacement with a clear plan built around motion, independence and realistic recovery.

Common hip conditions treated.

This page is starting with a stronger hero and will continue to expand into a full hip education hub.

Hip arthritis

Groin pain, stiffness, limping, loss of motion and trouble with walking, stairs or shoes and socks.

Lateral hip pain and bursitis

Pain on the outside of the hip, pain lying on the side and irritation with walking or stairs.

Hip tendinopathy

Gluteal tendon and soft-tissue pain that can mimic arthritis or spine-related symptoms.

Avascular necrosis evaluation

Evaluation of femoral head blood supply concerns and collapse risk when suspected.

Hip fractures

Fracture evaluation and surgical care when needed after falls or higher-energy injuries.

Pain after prior hip replacement

Evaluation of painful or failed prior hip replacement and referral/revision planning when appropriate.

Treatment options

Depending on the diagnosis, treatment may include activity modification, physical therapy, image-guided injections, anti-inflammatory care when appropriate, robotic-assisted total hip replacement, fracture care or revision evaluation.
