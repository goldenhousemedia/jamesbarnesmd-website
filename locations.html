Locations & Surgery Sites

Dr. Barnes sees patients at CH Orthopedics at Convergence Health in Lake Havasu City. When surgery is needed, the procedure setting is selected based on the patient, diagnosis, procedure, insurance, and safest plan for care.

Orthopedic care begins in clinic, then moves to the right care setting.

Most decisions start with a clinic evaluation: symptoms, exam, imaging review, prior treatment, patient goals, medical risk, and insurance requirements. If surgery is recommended, the facility is chosen around safety and appropriateness.

Outpatient Surgery

Convergence Ambulatory Surgery Center

Selected outpatient orthopedic procedures may be appropriate for an ambulatory surgery center setting. This depends on the procedure, medical history, anesthesia plan, insurance requirements, and overall safety.

Potential fitSelected arthroscopy, tendon, hand, and other outpatient procedures when clinically appropriate.

Hospital-Based Care

Havasu Regional Medical Center

Hospital-based orthopedic procedures may be used when a patient needs hospital resources, overnight capability, fracture care resources, or a setting that better matches the complexity of the case.

Common fitSelected hip, knee, shoulder replacement, fracture care, and higher-acuity orthopedic procedures.
Safety firstThe surgical setting should match the patient, diagnosis, procedure, and medical needs.
