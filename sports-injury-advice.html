Before, During, and After Activity

Practical habits that help athletes reduce injury risk, recognize early warning signs, and recover smarter.

Before Activity

  • Warm up gradually before high-speed or explosive movements.
  • Hydrate early, especially in Lake Havasu heat.
  • Use sport-appropriate footwear and protective equipment.
  • Build training volume gradually rather than making abrupt jumps.
  • Do mobility and activation work for the hips, shoulders, ankles, and core.
  • Do not ignore recurring pain that changes your mechanics.

During Activity

  • Stop for deformity, instability, sudden swelling, or inability to bear weight.
  • Respect heat symptoms: dizziness, chills, confusion, headache, or nausea.
  • Do not play through progressive weakness, numbness, or sharp joint pain.
  • Use good technique when lifting, cutting, landing, or throwing.
  • Rotate positions and movement patterns when possible to reduce overuse.

After Activity

  • Cool down and restore motion after intense exercise.
  • Prioritize sleep, protein intake, hydration, and active recovery.
  • Address soreness that lasts longer than expected or worsens each session.
  • Use ice, compression, elevation, and short-term activity modification when needed.
  • Seek evaluation when symptoms affect sleep, work, walking, lifting, or sport.

Dr. Barnes' Common-Sense Rules

  • Pain that changes how you move deserves attention.
  • Swelling after activity is information, not an inconvenience.
  • Do not rush back from ligament injuries, tendon ruptures, or surgery.
  • Strength without control is not readiness.
  • Return to activity should be progressive, not all-or-nothing.

Lake Havasu Heat & Hydration

Desert training adds risk. Athletes should plan hydration, electrolytes, shade, rest intervals, sun protection, and realistic training times. Heat illness can become dangerous quickly and should be treated seriously.

Red flag: confusion, collapse, persistent vomiting, severe headache, or altered mental status after heat exposure requires urgent/emergency evaluation.
